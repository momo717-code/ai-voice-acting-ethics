# 🎙️ AI & Voice Acting — An Ethics Study

A presentation examining the **ethics of AI voice cloning and synthetic voice acting**: what the technology can do, where it helps, where it harms, and why the law hasn't caught up. A study in responsible and consensual use of generative AI.

## What it covers

**The technology.** Modern systems can clone a voice from **3–10 seconds** of audio, then generate entirely new lines that mimic a person's identity, tone, and emotion — used across films, games, dubbing, ads, and audiobooks, sometimes **without consent**.

**The case *for*.** Creative and accessibility benefits: preserving or extending performances (e.g. licensed iconic voices), lowering barriers for new storytellers, and restoring voices for people who have lost them. *"It's not about replacing voices; it's about amplifying them, opening doors for new storytellers everywhere."* — attributed to Sir Michael Caine.

**The case *against*.** Consent, identity, and livelihood: performers being imitated without permission or payment, and public-figure deepfakes. Referenced examples include disputes and controversies around David Attenborough deepfakes, the Scarlett Johansson / synthetic-voice episode, and AI "actors" such as Tilly Norwood. *"Don't mimic me with falseness… if you're gonna do it without me, you're robbing me."* — attributed to Morgan Freeman.

**The legal gap.** Misuse of voice cloning, the absence of clear laws, and high-profile deepfake incidents (e.g. the fake Biden robocall) — and the question of **who this actually affects**: actors, public figures, and ordinary people alike.

**Conclusion.** The creative and accessibility upside is real, but so is the risk to consent, identity, and livelihoods. The takeaway is a call for **consent, fair compensation, and regulation** to keep pace with the technology.

## Why it's here

Beyond coursework, this reflects an active interest in **AI safety, governance, and the responsible deployment of generative models** — the questions of consent, misuse, and accountability that sit at the centre of trustworthy AI.
